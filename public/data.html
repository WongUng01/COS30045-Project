Data Sources

OECD

The OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) provides comprehensive environmental statistics, including data on air quality, emissions, and other critical indicators. These datasets are valuable for understanding environmental trends and policy impacts across member countries.

World Bank

The World Bank offers extensive global development data, including information on environmental sustainability, health outcomes, and economic development. This source is instrumental in connecting air quality data with socioeconomic indicators to uncover meaningful relationships.

Our World in Data

Our World in Data provides accessible and interactive visualizations on various global issues, including air pollution, CO2 emissions, and fossil fuel consumption. This source allows for deep insights into historical trends and their impact on public health and the environment.

Processed Datasets

The datasets used in this project have undergone careful curation and processing to ensure they align with the objectives of our study. Each dataset has been meticulously cleaned, structured, and enriched to provide the most accurate and relevant insights into air quality trends and their impacts on public health and the environment. Data preprocessing steps included handling missing values, ensuring consistency across different formats, and normalizing variables to enable accurate comparisons and visualizations. Each dataset included information from reputable sources such as the OECD, World Bank, and Our World in Data, providing a comprehensive perspective on issues like PM2.5 exposure, fossil fuel consumption, and CO2 emissions from transport.

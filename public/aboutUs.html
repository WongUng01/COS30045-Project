Welcome to our project, where we delve into the critical issue of air quality and its profound implications on health and sustainability. Our initiative focuses on understanding the impact of PM2.5—a fine particulate matter that poses significant risks to both human well-being and the environment. By harnessing the power of data visualization, we aim to uncover the complex dynamics of air pollution and inspire actionable solutions for a healthier future.


Our Mission

Our mission is to bridge the gap between environmental data and public understanding. We focus on examining Malaysia’s air quality within the broader context of global industrial and economic development. By analyzing trends, comparing emission levels, and exploring the health and economic impacts of air quality, we aim to contribute to informed decision-making and a sustainable future.


What We Do

Our project explores the intricate relationship between industrialization, air quality, and public health. Key areas of focus include:

  • Tracking and comparing PM2.5 levels across developed and developing nations.
  • Exploring how nations transition from industrial growth to cleaner technologies.
  • Highlighting the economic and health consequences of air pollution.
  • Identifying major sources of PM2.5 emissions in Malaysia.
  • Creating interactive and accessible data visualizations to engage and educate the public.

Our Vision

Through this work, we envision a future where Malaysia and other developing nations achieve cleaner air, healthier communities, and sustainable growth. By learning from the successes of developed countries and embracing cutting-edge technologies, we believe it is possible to balance industrialization with environmental responsibility.
